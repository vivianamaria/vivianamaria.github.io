Carboxylic Acids

Carboxylic acids are a simple class of molecules with an overall rod-like shape. They show liquid crystalline properties and they self-assembly to form larger, well-organized architectures based solely on their design.

Phases of 4-alkoxybenzoic acid

For example, 4-alkoxybenzoic acids, self-assemble in two ways. First, hydrogen bonding between the carboxylic acid groups leads to the formation of a dimer with a pronounced rod-like shape (Figure 1). Second, these rod-like dimers form one or more liquid crystal phases (Figures 2 and 3) that can be seen by simply observing the materials on heating as they pass through their various phase transitions.

Each 4-alkoxybenzoic acid has a unique liquid crystal phase. The liquid crystal phase is temperature dependent. The various chain length has distinguishable liquid crystal phase transitions that can be observed using polarizing optical microscopy (POM).
